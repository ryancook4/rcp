    Fellow/Co-Chair

    During the first semester of his freshman year, Ryan took ND's "God and the Good Life" introductory philosophy course as a University requirement but grew to love the course. He began working with the course as a Fellow the following semester and has spent the past 3+ years working to further the course's mission of contemplating life's bigger questions and making philosophy pragmatic to the lives of college students. Ryan has planned and led dialogue sessions of 10-15 students every Tuesday night in which students practice critical thinking and logical discourse on topics ranging from Kant's categorical imperative to Thanos' moral agenda. After his second semester with GGL, Ryan undertook the role of Co-Chair of the undergraduate teaching team to work more closely with course instructors on coordinating course logistics and organizing the growing team of course Fellows.

God and the Good Life
    Student/Speaker

    In the Fall '19, Ryan was a student in Notre Dame's first and only module of The Good Class. Inspired by Michael Schur's hit NBC comedy The Good Place, the course combined TV scripts and reviews with age-old philosophical ideas on morality to examine if TV can make us better people. Through weekly readings and group dialogues with professors, remote interviews with show consultants, and a luncheon/presentation day with Mike Schur himself, students had the unique opportunity to gain unique insight into The Good Place's and its impact on socio-cultural ideas of morality during the show's final season. For their final project in the course, Ryan and Chase collaborated with their high school Theology teacher Tom Healey to design an alumni lecture for seniors in Healey's formative "Christian Manhood" course at St. Ignatius High School in Cleveland. Chase and Ryan introduced the high school students to the main classical concepts of morality, analyzed an episode of The Good Place, and fielded questions on how their Jesuit high school prepared them for handling important life decisions in college.

Aside from the incredible community, Ryan's favorite aspect of Fisher Hall is the Fisher Regatta, an event in which students race homemade boats across St. Mary's Lake in support of St. Adalbert School on the west side of South Bend. Since last year's event was cancelled due to the pandemic, the Regatta needs more help than ever this spring. Please check out the Fisher Regatta Facebook page and consider donating to its GoFundMe!
